Equations

There are a number of ways to formulate the equation of a triangle wave. Commonly the floor function or mod function are used in addition to an absolute value.

With the mod function, the equation is \[f\left(x\right)=\frac{4}{p}\left|{(x+\phi)\mod p}-\frac{p}{2}\right|-1 \tag{1}\] where

  • \(p\) is the period (unitless, it corresponds directly to the x-axis)
  • \(phi\) is the phase (also unitless)

With the floor function, the equation is \[g\left(x\right)=4\left|\frac{x+\phi}{p}-\lfloor(\frac{x+\phi}{p})\rfloor-\frac{1}{2}\right|-1 \tag{2}\]
